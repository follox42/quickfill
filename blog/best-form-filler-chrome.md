# 5 Best Form Filler Extensions for Chrome in 2026

Filling out forms is one of those tasks that eats more time than anyone wants to admit. Whether you're a developer testing login flows, a recruiter submitting applications, or just someone tired of typing their address for the hundredth time, a form filler extension is one of the highest-ROI tools you can install.

But which one? The Chrome Web Store has dozens of form fillers, and most of them are either outdated, overpriced, or borderline spyware. I spent two weeks testing the most popular options across real-world scenarios: e-commerce checkouts, multi-step registration forms, React-based SPAs, and government forms with weird field types.

Here are the 5 worth considering, ranked.

## 1. QuickFill -- Best Overall (Free)

**[QuickFill](https://quickfill.pages.dev)** is the newest entry on this list, and it immediately earned the top spot.

Built from scratch in 2025, QuickFill doesn't carry the baggage of extensions designed a decade ago. It was built with modern web frameworks in mind, which means it actually works on React, Vue, and Angular forms -- something that trips up almost every other extension on this list.

**Pros:**
- Completely free -- all features, no premium tier, no subscription
- 3 customizable profiles for different contexts (personal, work, testing)
- Handles dropdowns, checkboxes, radio buttons, and date pickers
- Framework-aware: triggers proper change events for React/Vue/Angular
- Keyboard shortcuts for fast filling
- Privacy-first: no accounts, no servers, data stored locally in Chrome
- Clean, modern interface

**Cons:**
- Limited to 3 profiles (enough for most users, but power users may want more)
- Newer extension, so the community is still growing
- No conditional filling rules (yet)

**Best for:** Developers, testers, job seekers, and anyone who wants a reliable free form filler that works on modern websites.

## 2. Lightning Autofill -- Best for Power Users (Paid)

Lightning Autofill dominated this category for years. It earned its reputation with reliable field detection and a robust feature set. Then it went paid in 2025, and the free tier became barely usable.

**Pros:**
- Mature, battle-tested extension with years of development
- Unlimited profiles on paid plans
- Conditional filling rules (fill field A based on field B's value)
- Import/export functionality
- Regular expression support for field matching

**Cons:**
- Free tier limited to basic text fields only -- dropdowns, checkboxes, and dates require payment
- $5-8/month subscription for full features
- Inconsistent behavior on React/Vue apps
- UI feels dated compared to newer alternatives
- Privacy policy is vague about data handling

**Best for:** Enterprise users and power users who need conditional logic and unlimited profiles, and don't mind paying for it.

---

## 3. Fake Filler -- Best for Random Test Data

Fake Filler takes a different approach. Instead of filling forms with your real data, it generates random data -- random names, emails, addresses, phone numbers. This makes it a favorite among QA testers who need to submit forms hundreds of times with varied data.

**Pros:**
- Generates realistic random data for every field type
- Useful for stress-testing forms and finding edge cases
- Free and open source
- No configuration needed -- just click and fill
- Good for privacy-conscious testing (no real data exposed)

**Cons:**
- Not designed for filling forms with your actual information
- Limited customization -- you can't save specific profiles
- Random data can trigger validation errors on strict forms
- Doesn't handle all modern framework components well
- No keyboard shortcuts

**Best for:** QA testers and developers who need random, varied form data for testing rather than consistent personal data.

---

## 4. AutoFill Forms -- Solid but Aging

AutoFill Forms has been around for a long time. It gets the basics right and has a loyal user base. But its age is showing.

**Pros:**
- Free for basic functionality
- Straightforward setup -- enter your data, fill forms
- Lightweight and doesn't slow down your browser
- Works on standard HTML forms reliably

**Cons:**
- UI looks like it was designed in 2012
- Struggles with modern JavaScript frameworks
- Dropdown and date field support is inconsistent
- Limited profile management
- Development appears slow -- long gaps between updates
- Some users report it stops working after Chrome updates

**Best for:** Users who only fill simple HTML forms and want something lightweight.

---

## 5. Web Developer Form Filler -- Minimalist Option

A stripped-down form filler that targets web developers specifically. It's minimal by design, which is both its strength and limitation.

**Pros:**
- Extremely lightweight
- Open source
- Quick to set up
- Good for developers who want to understand exactly what the extension does

**Cons:**
- Very limited field type support
- No profiles -- single data set only
- No dropdown or checkbox support
- Manual configuration required for non-standard fields
- Small user base, limited support

**Best for:** Developers who want the most minimal possible form filler and are comfortable with limitations.
